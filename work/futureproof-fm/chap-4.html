4  Bonds

4.1 Supplementary Concepts

Notation
\(P\) Price (i.e., present value of cash flows)
\(F\) Face amount or par value
\(C\) Redemption amount
\(r\) coupon rate
\(Fr\) Coupon amount
\(g=\cfrac{Fr}{C}\) Adjusted coupon rate
\(i\) Yield rate (or internal rate of return)
\(n\) Number of coupon payment periods
\(P-C\) Premium (if \(P>C \Leftrightarrow i<g)\)
\(C-P\) Discount (if \(C>P \Leftrightarrow i>g)\)

  1. Bonds are loans that investors make to corporations or governments, where the borrower (issuer) promises to repay the principal (face value) of the loan at a specified future date (maturity) and also pay periodic interest payments (coupons) to the bondholder. Bonds are a fixed-income investment, meaning they offer a predictable stream of income through interest payments.

  2. The following notation will be used in this unit:

  3. The main pricing formulas are: \[ \boxed{ \begin{array}{rl} \\ \quad P &= Fr\,a_{\enclose{actuarial}{n}}+Cv^n & \text{(basic} \,\, \text{formula)} \quad\\ \\ \quad P &= C+(Fr-Ci)\,a_{\enclose{actuarial}{n}} & \text{(premium-discount formula)} \quad \\ \\ \end{array} } \]

  4. Callable bonds are bonds in which the borrower has an option to redeem prior to its maturity date. If \(C_t=C\) , \(\forall t\), then the following rules apply:

  • If \(i<g\) (i.e., the bond sells at a premium), assume that the redemption date will be the earliest possible date
  • If \(i>g\) (i.e., the bond sells at a discount), assume that the redemption date will be the latest possible date

  1. Preferred stock is a fixed-income security without a redemption date. In this case, \(P=\cfrac{Fr}{i}\).

  2. Common stock is a variable-income security. In theory, its price should represent the present value of future dividends. If \(D\) is a dividend that is projected to change geometrically with common ratio \(1+k\), then \(P=\cfrac{D}{i-k}\)

4.2 Solved Exercises

Before opening the solutions, take a moment to work through each problem—-your mastery grows most when you engage actively with the mathematics.

Bond schedules

A \(10{,000}\) par value ten-year bond with a coupon rate of \(10\%\) payable semiannually and redeemable at par is bought to yield \(12\%\) convertible semiannually.

Find

  1. the price of the bond
  2. the accumulation of discount in the fifth coupon date
  3. the total accumulation of discount
  4. the accumulation of discount in the first five years
  5. the total interest paid
  6. the interest paid in years \(6-10\)
NoteSolution
  1. Since the coupons are paid semiannualy, we will work in semesters. Let \(P\) be the price of the bond. Then

\[ \begin{aligned} P & =C+(Fr-Ci)\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{20}}\\ &= 1{,}000 + \left[ 10{,}000(0.05) - 10{,}000(0.06)\right]\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{20}}\\ &= 8{,}853.01 \end{aligned} \]

  1. The accumulation of discount in the \(k^{th}\) coupon date is given by \(-(B_{k-1}-B_k)\). Thus,

\[ \begin{aligned} -B_4+B_5 & = -\left[ C+(Fr-Ci)\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{16}} \right] + \left[ C+(Fr-Ci)\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{15}} \right] \\ &= (Fr-Ci)\, (a_{\enclose{actuarial}{15}} - a_{\enclose{actuarial}{16}}) \\ &= (Fr-Ci)\, \cfrac{v^{16} - v^{15}}{i} \\ &= [10{,}000(0.05)-10{,}000(0.06)]\, \cfrac{v^{16} - v^{15}}{i} \\ &= 589.35 \end{aligned} \]

  1. Total accumulation of discount is given by \(C-P=10{,}000-8{,}853.01=1{,}146.99\).

  2. The accumulation of discount in the first five years is given by \(-(B_0-B_{10})\). But \(B_0=P=8{,}853.01\). Thus,

\[ \begin{aligned} B_{10} - 8{,}853.01 &= C + (Fr-Ci)\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{10}} - 8{,}853.01 \\ &= 10{,}000 + [10{,}000(0.05)-10{,}000(0.06)]\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{10}} - 8{,}853.01 \\ &= 410.98 \end{aligned} \]

  1. Total interest paid is given by \(\sum \text{coupons} + \text{total acc of discount}\). Thus,

\[ \begin{aligned} 20(10{,}000)(0.05) + 1{,}146.99 = 11{,}146.99 \end{aligned} \]

  1. Interest paid in years \(6-10\) is given by \(\sum_{i=11}^{20} \text{coupons} + \sum_{i=11}^{20}\text{acc of discount}\,_i\). Thus,

\[ \begin{aligned} 10(10{,}000)(0.05) + \sum_{i=11}^{20}\text{acc of discount}\,_i &= 5{,}000 + [-B_{10}+B_{20}] \\ &= 5{,}000 -B_{10}+10,000 \\ &= 15{,}000 - [C + (Fr-Ci)\, a_{\enclose{actuarial}{10}}] \\ &= 5{,}736.01 \end{aligned} \]

\(\\\)

4.4 Code snippets

Newton-Raphson example to calculate the interest rate of a bond:

Let \(f(j)\) represent the price of a bond with \(F=1\,000\), \(C=1\,100\), \(n=24\), and \(r=0.05\).

The following algorithm finds the root of \(f(j)\) with \(│f(x)│ \le 10^{-8}\)

x0      <- 0.06
epsilon <- 10e-08

nr <- function(ini, eps){
  
  f   <- function(j) 1100 - 50 * ((1 - (1 + j)^-24) / j) - 1000 * (1 + j)^-24  # original function
  fpr <- function(j) -50 * ((j * (24 * (1 + j)^-25) - (1 + (1 + j)^-24)) / j^2) + 20000 * (1 + j)^-25   # first derivative
  
  xn1 <- ini - (f(ini) / fpr(ini))
  print(xn1)
  if(abs(xn1 - ini) > eps) nr(xn1, eps)
  
}
